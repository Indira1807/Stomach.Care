Detecting Stomach Diseases!

Stomach.Care | September 29, 2024

How to Detect Stomach Diseases

Stomach diseases are a common health issue that can cause various disturbing symptoms. The stomach plays a vital role in the digestive system, so when there are issues with this organ, it can affect overall body health. Early detection of stomach diseases is crucial for timely treatment and to prevent more serious complications. Here are several ways to detect stomach diseases.

1. Pay Attention to Symptoms

Common symptoms indicating stomach issues include:

  • Heartburn: Caused by stomach acid rising into the esophagus.
  • Nausea or vomiting: Occurs due to irritation of the stomach.
  • Bloating and feeling of fullness in the abdomen: Usually occurs after eating.
  • Upper abdominal pain: Pain often arises after meals or at night.
  • Difficulties swallowing: A sign of a serious problem in the esophagus.

2. Medical History and Physical Examination

The doctor will conduct a medical interview and physical examination to detect physical signs of stomach disorders, such as tenderness when touching the abdominal area.

3. Laboratory Tests

Some laboratory tests performed to detect stomach problems include:

  • Blood tests: To detect the presence of Helicobacter pylori infection.
  • Stool tests: Help detect infections or other digestive issues.

4. Endoscopy

Endoscopy is performed by inserting a small camera-equipped tool to view the stomach's condition directly, checking for gastritis, ulcers, or GERD.

5. Urea Breath Test

This test detects the presence of Helicobacter pylori bacteria by measuring carbon dioxide gas in the breath after the patient ingests a urea-containing liquid.

6. Esophageal pH Test

This test measures the acidity level in the esophagus, helping to diagnose GERD.

7. Esophageal Manometry

This test evaluates the muscle contractions in the esophagus to determine if there are issues affecting the swallowing process.

Prevention and Early Management

After detection, some steps that can be taken to prevent stomach diseases from worsening include:

  • Dietary changes: Avoid spicy, fatty, and acidic foods.
  • Avoid alcohol and tobacco: Both substances can worsen stomach symptoms.
  • Use medications as prescribed by your doctor: Antacids or other medications may be prescribed to address symptoms.

Conclusion

Early detection of stomach diseases is crucial to prevent serious complications. Consult a doctor promptly if you experience symptoms related to the stomach to ensure accurate diagnosis and treatment.
